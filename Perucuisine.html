You may not know it but boastng three of the world's top one hundred restaurants Peru is a gastronomy capital. You will find food that is simple and yet complex, modern yet traditional, full of flavour and vivid colour. The adventure of Peru is not only in the mountains of the Andes but right on your plate.

Maido - Lima

Located in Lima's trendy Miraflores district, Maido is paving the way for fusion cuisine in Peru. Head chef Mitsuharu 'Micha' Tsumura blends beautiful traditional flavours of Peru and Japan and the Nikkei Experience menu transports diners to the depths of the ocean.

Chica - Cusco

You dont have to be in trendy Lima to experience Peru's fine dining circuit. Chicha is a culinary delight from famous Pervian chef Gaston Acurio in the Inca captical of Cusco. Overlooking a beautiful central plaza the menu focuses on hearty trditional dishes with a modern twist. The octopus is divine and try the ox tongue to keep it local.

Ayahuasca Resto Bar - Lima

In the art district of Barranco, Lima you will find this quirky bar serving delicious traditional plates. Set within a colonial-style mansion with inventive decor expect to find typical dishes to share such as salchipapa and Tacu Tacu. Perfect for a laidback feast with gret cocktails.

Must eat - Cuy

Yes, you guessed it... guinea pig! A delicacy in Peru you cant leave without trying it. Idigenous to the Andean region, you will find cuy on most restaurants across Peru. With a rich flavour akin to Gammon it's a treat and best of all its high in protein and low in cholestoral!

Must eat - Fried fish and Ceviche

Peruvian Food Markets

No need to book ahead to enjoy some of Peru's best food. The food markets across the country are a bustling hive of locals feasting on traditional fare. Delicous stews and roasted foul, mouth watering aromas fill the air. Those of a nervous disposition may want to avoid the face soup though!
